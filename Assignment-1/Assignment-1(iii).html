LED VS. LCD

LED:

LED (Light-Emitting Diode) is a semiconductor device that emits light when an electric current passes through it. It is energy-efficient, has a long lifespan, and is durable. LEDs are commonly used for lighting in various applications, from electronic devices to household and automotive lighting.

LCD:

LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) is a flat-panel display technology used in devices like TVs, monitors, smartphones, and tablets. It consists of two layers of polarized glass with a liquid crystal solution between them. Electric currents control the alignment of liquid crystals, producing images and text on the screen.
